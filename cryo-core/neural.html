  • neurology
    Self-Learning ML Model The onboard neural engine runs a lightweight self-learning model trained on workload signatures. It continuously updates its weights to predict heat spikes with high confidence, proactively adjusting pump speed and fan curves before CPU temperatures spike — eliminating reactive throttling entirely.
  • sensors
    Multi-Point Telemetry Six embedded temperature sensors, a workload-activity monitor, and an ambient environmental sensor feed data into the neural engine at 10 ms intervals. This real-time telemetry underpins the model's ability to distinguish gaming bursts, rendering jobs, and idle states with millisecond precision.
  • auto_awesome
    Continuous Self-Optimisation After each session the model re-evaluates its predictions against actual thermal outcomes. Missed predictions trigger weight adjustments so the system becomes progressively quieter, more efficient, and more accurate the longer it runs on your specific hardware and usage pattern.
