Cooling Poverty

High-end users own powerful hardware, but air cooling leaves them with fan noise, dust buildup, neglected maintenance, and up to 40% performance loss from throttling.

water_drop

Dielectric Immersion

Components are submerged in non-conductive dielectric fluid, moving heat away faster than air while allowing the system to run quietly and safely.

health_and_safety

Protocol First

The presentation centers safety training: unplug equipment before opening tanks, use gloves above 150°F, and disconnect main power before servicing.

Training & Service

Safety-Led Deployment Support

The service model includes engineering training for fluid depths, volumetric measurements, molecular heat-transfer concepts, weekly routines, and strict caution, warning, and danger notices.

24-Week Deployment Roadmap

  1. 01

    Weeks 1-6

    Prepping: baseline systems, unbox primary gear, and set up the working environment for immersion testing.

  2. 02

    Weeks 7-12

    Prototype integration: technicians build the first submerged cooling prototype together.

  3. 03

    Weeks 13-18

    Load balancing: stress-test the system under heavy workloads and verify stable thermal performance.

  4. 04

    Weeks 19-24

    Deployment: complete final testing and prepare the official product launch.
